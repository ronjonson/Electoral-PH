Philippine Electoral College
This website shows a representative map and results of the Philippines during each Presidential and Vice-Presidential Election if it adopted the US Electoral College
Each year is divided into the Presidential and Vice-Presidential Elections.

How the Electoral College Works
The Electoral College is the system used in the United States of America to elect their President
According usa.gov's page on the electoral college
the President and Vice-President are elected through electors. Each state gets as many electors as it has members of congress
The winner of the popular vote for each state gets all the electors assigned to it.
For Example, if the winner of a certain state wins the popular vote by 40%, they will still get 100% of the electors for that certain state
To win the electoral college, you have to win at least 50% of the number of electors

In the Philippines, the number of representatives changes year by year, so the number of electors will change too
In this case, I have assumed that each Province and Independent City each get their own electors which amount to the number of Representatives they send to the Batasang Pambansa
I have also only counted the district representatives and not party list representatives as they dont representate any specific locale
The Senate has also been disregarded in the counting due to the fact that the Senate does not represent any specific area
Independent cities and Highly Urbanized Cities, (who have their own representatives) are administered separately from the province they are found in and therefore considered a separate 'state' in this map
Some Independent cities do not have their own representatives (such as Angeles City) and therefore were not separated from their province in this case
